# Quantum Mechanics Fundamentals

## Introduction
The Schrödinger equation forms the foundation of quantum mechanics.

## Key Concepts
- **Wave Function**: $\psi(x,t)$ represents the quantum state
- **Schrödinger Equation**: $i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\psi = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2\psi}{\partial x^2} + V\psi$
- **Expectation Values**: $\langle x \rangle = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \psi^* x \psi dx$
- **Uncertainty**: $\Delta x \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$

## Applications
The wave equation describes particle behavior at quantum scales:
$$
\begin{pmatrix} 
\frac{\partial}{\partial t} & -c\frac{\partial}{\partial x} \\
-c\frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial t}
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
\phi_1 \\ \phi_2
\end{pmatrix} = 0
$$

---
**Summary**: Quantum mechanics uses wave functions and operators to describe microscopic systems.
